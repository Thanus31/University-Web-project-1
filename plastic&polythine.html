- Plastic and polythine -

Plastic and other waste have significant negative effects on the environment in Sri Lanka. Pollution from plastic waste, particularly single-use items like bags and bottles, contaminates water bodies, including rivers, lakes, and oceans, harming marine and freshwater ecosystems and contaminating drinking water supplies. Coastal areas suffer from the unsightly accumulation of plastic debris, endangering wildlife through ingestion and entanglement. Improper disposal also leads to soil contamination, affecting agricultural lands and potentially causing health hazards. Air pollution from waste incineration exacerbates respiratory problems, particularly in urban areas. The tourism industry is impacted by litter-strewn beaches and polluted water, deterring tourists and tarnishing the country's image. Moreover, improper waste management poses public health risks, with waste serving as breeding grounds for disease vectors and burning plastic emitting toxic fumes. Addressing these challenges requires coordinated efforts to implement effective waste management strategies, promote recycling and reuse, and raise awareness about the importance of reducing plastic consumption.





How we can save the enviroment from plastic and polythine

Action Discription
Reduce Plastic Usage Use alternatives like reusable bags, bottles, and containers.
Recycle Separate plastic waste for recycling where possible.
Proper Disposal Dispose of plastic waste in designated bins or recycling centers.
Avoid Single-Use Plastics Choose products with minimal or no plastic packaging.
Support Bans Advocate for and support policies banning single-use plastics.
Participate in Cleanups Join community clean-up efforts to remove plastic from the environment.
Educate Others Spread awareness about the impact of plastic pollution and encourage sustainable practices.
