- Improper waste management -

Improper waste management in Sri Lanka poses significant environmental and public health challenges. With rapid urbanization and population growth, the country grapples with inadequate infrastructure and enforcement mechanisms to handle its escalating waste generation. Many areas lack proper waste disposal facilities, leading to indiscriminate dumping in water bodies, open spaces, and along roadsides. This not only contaminates soil and water sources but also contributes to the proliferation of diseases. The absence of comprehensive recycling and composting programs further exacerbates the problem, as valuable resources are lost, and organic waste continues to burden landfills. Urgent action is required to invest in sustainable waste management solutions, including infrastructure development, public awareness campaigns, and policy reforms to mitigate the adverse impacts of improper waste disposal in Sri Lanka.

    Education and Awareness
      Promote education programs to raise awareness about the importance of proper waste disposal methods and the consequences of improper waste management.
    Infrastructure Development
      Invest in the development of waste management infrastructure such as recycling facilities, composting plants, and waste-to-energy facilities to reduce the amount of waste sent to landfills.
    Enforcement of Regulations
      Implement and enforce strict regulations and penalties for illegal dumping and improper waste disposal practices to deter offenders and ensure compliance.
    Community Engagement
      Encourage community participation through initiatives such as clean-up campaigns, recycling drives, and neighborhood composting programs to foster a sense of responsibility and ownership towards waste management
