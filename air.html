- Air pollution -

Air pollution in Sri Lanka poses serious health and environmental risks due to urbanization, industrial growth, and vehicular emissions. Major cities like Colombo suffer from high levels of pollutants such as particulate matter and nitrogen oxides, worsened by traffic congestion and industrial activities. Inefficient waste management practices, like open burning of garbage, exacerbate the problem. This pollution leads to respiratory illnesses, cardiovascular diseases, and harms ecosystems and agriculture. Tackling this issue demands stricter vehicle emission regulations, better public transport, cleaner industrial methods, and awareness campaigns promoting sustainable practices.


To tackle air pollution in Sri Lanka, several measures must be implemented. Firstly, the government should enforce strict regulations on vehicle emissions by conducting regular inspections and imposing penalties for non-compliance. Additionally, promoting the use of public transportation, cycling, and walking can reduce the number of vehicles on the road, thus decreasing pollution levels. Furthermore, investing in renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power can mitigate the reliance on fossil fuels, which are major contributors to air pollution. Educating the public about the harmful effects of air pollution and encouraging eco-friendly practices such as tree planting and waste reduction can also foster a culture of environmental responsibility. By implementing these strategies, Sri Lanka can make significant strides towards combating air pollution and ensuring a healthier environment for future generations.

Here are some of the main air pollution problems in Sri Lanka along with potential solutions

    Vehicle emissions -
      Promote electric/hybrid vehicles, improve public transportation, enforce emission standards.
    Industrial pollution -
      Install pollution control tech, monitor/enforce emission standards, encourage cleaner production processes.
    Biomass burning -
      Promote cleaner cooking tech, proper waste management, sustainable agricultural practices.
    Deforestation and land use changes -
      Enforce regulations against deforestation, promote reforestation, encourage sustainable land management.
